Patrick Billy – Official Biography

Patrick Billy is a French designer of inclusive digital tools, specializing in complex, constrained, or low-infrastructure environments. He works at the intersection of digital accessibility, inclusion, work analysis, and applied artificial intelligence.

With a background in graphic design, training, and field-based design, Patrick develops tools that adapt to people — not the other way around. His philosophy is straightforward: simple, robust, and genuinely useful solutions for teachers, employees, people with disabilities, families, and any organization facing real-world situations.

Élan pour Tous

He founded Élan pour Tous based on a clear observation: many technologies are designed for idealized environments, while real-world conditions are often complex, fragmented, and unpredictable. Patrick therefore creates tools that function without internet access, without expensive hardware, and without unnecessary complexity.

Mini-WTD (Work-Test-Demo)

Patrick is also the creator of Mini-WTD, a simulator designed to analyze work situations. It allows tasks to be examined, constraints to be understood, feasibility to be evaluated, and concrete workplace adaptations to be proposed.

Mini-WTD is now used in various contexts: schools, companies, medical-social institutions, independent trainers, and low-tech environments such as overseas territories or areas with limited connectivity.

Areas of Expertise

  • Digital accessibility (RGAA / WCAG)
  • Inclusive design & simplified interfaces
  • Applied AI for explanation, guidance, and simplification
  • Low-tech and offline tools (USB solutions, intranet, constrained contexts)
  • Activity analysis and task simulation
  • Workplace adaptation & inclusive pedagogy

Working Between France and Madagascar

Patrick primarily works in France, but also develops tools adapted to Malagasy and broader African realities, where digital infrastructures are often limited. Understanding real-world situations is central to his approach: he designs tools that operate where conventional solutions fail.

Philosophy

His entire work is based on one conviction: technology should reduce cognitive and operational load, not increase it.

His tools are designed for people who may not like technology, who are tired, overwhelmed, or facing challenging circumstances. This is precisely where simplicity becomes strength.
